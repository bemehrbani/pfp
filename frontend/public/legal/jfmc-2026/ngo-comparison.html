2. Strategic Analysis

🇫🇮 Finland Strengths

  • Fastest and cheapest setup in the EU
  • Strongest rule of law rankings globally
  • Reliable MFA funding for human rights NGOs, unaffected by political shifts
  • Democratic, member-based governance (association model)
  • EU-compliant legal framework

🇳🇱 Netherlands Strengths

  • Home to ICC and ICJ — physical proximity to the courts that matter
  • 2,500+ justice NGOs already operating from The Hague
  • No residency requirement for directors
  • ANBI status provides structured tax benefits + donor deductibility
  • Brand credibility — "The Hague" is synonymous with international justice

3. Recommendation

🏆 Primary Entity: Netherlands (Stichting)

For an NGO whose core mission is legal research and documentation for international court proceedings, the Netherlands is the optimal jurisdiction. The proximity to ICC/ICJ, the established justice ecosystem, ANBI tax benefits, and no residency requirement create the strongest foundation for this work.

The Hague isn't just a convenient location — it's the center of gravity for international justice. Being physically present means:

  • Direct engagement with the ICC Office of the Prosecutor
  • Access to international law experts and counsel
  • Networking with peer organizations (TRIAL International, Global Rights Compliance, PAX)
  • Participation in Assembly of States Parties meetings
  • Media visibility in the default city for international justice coverage
Dual-entity strategy: Consider establishing in both jurisdictions — Netherlands as the primary legal entity for international court engagement, and Finland as a secondary entity for stable MFA research funding and EU-wide advocacy.

5. Netherlands Setup Checklist

🇳🇱 Stichting — Action Items
  1. Find a Dutch notary experienced with international NGOs (The Hague preferred)
  2. Draft Articles of Association — purpose: legal research, evidence documentation, international advocacy for victims of armed conflict
  3. Secure Dutch registered address — virtual office (€100–€300/yr) or partner organization
  4. Identify 3 board members — no residency requirement
  5. Execute notarial deed (€500–€1,500) → KVK registration (2–3 days)
  6. Apply for ANBI status with Belastingdienst immediately after registration
  7. Open bank account — ABN AMRO or ING; prepare comprehensive KYC package
  8. Publish required ANBI information on website (statutes, policy plan, board members)
  9. Join The Hague Justice Portal and connect with Coalition for the ICC

6. Finland Setup Checklist

🇫🇮 Association — Action Items
  1. Identify Finnish-resident chairperson (or apply for PRH exemption)
  2. Find 3+ founders (natural persons 15+ or legal entities)
  3. Draft association rules (säännöt) in Finnish — legal translation service
  4. Hold founding meeting → adopt rules, elect board, sign charter
  5. Register with PRH — €55 online / €110 paper (2 working days)
  6. Open bank account — OP, Nordea, or S-Pankki
  7. Apply for MFA funding in next grant cycle
  8. Build partnership with FHRA (Finnish Human Rights Association) and ECCHR

§ 1–2: Name, Domicile & Purpose

§ 1 — Name and Domicile

People for Peace & Justice ry

The name of the association is People for Peace & Justice ry.

The association's domicile is Helsinki, Finland.

§ 2 — Purpose (Tarkoitus)

Mission & Purpose Clause

The association's purpose is to advance international justice and accountability for human rights violations against children and civilians, with particular focus on state violence.

The association fulfills its purpose by:

§ 2 — Purpose Activities (English Draft)
  1. Legal research and documentation of human rights violations, war crimes, and crimes against humanity, with emphasis on evidence-gathering methodologies that meet international legal standards.
  2. Advocacy and public awareness campaigns to inform the international community, governments, and judicial bodies about documented violations and the need for accountability.
  3. Support for victims and affected communities by facilitating access to legal remedies, connecting victims with legal representation, and coordinating with international judicial mechanisms.
  4. Engagement with international justice institutions including but not limited to the International Criminal Court (ICC), the International Court of Justice (ICJ), and UN treaty bodies.
  5. Knowledge sharing and capacity building through publications, educational programs, and partnerships with peer organizations in the international justice ecosystem.
  6. Diaspora engagement and community empowerment by connecting affected diaspora communities with documentation, legal, and advocacy resources.
  7. All other lawful means directly or indirectly conducive to the realization of the association's objectives.
Scope Design: Intentionally broad to allow work on multiple cases beyond the initial Minab case, engagement with any judicial body, and partnership flexibility — avoiding costly rule amendments later.

§ 3–5: Membership, Fees & Board

§ 3 — Membership (Jäsenyys)

Open Membership

Any natural person or legal entity that accepts the association's purpose may apply for membership. The board decides on accepting new members.

A member may resign at any time by written notice to the board. The board may expel a member who acts against the association's purpose or fails to pay membership fees.

§ 4 — Membership Fee (Jäsenmaksu)

Annual Fees

Members pay an annual membership fee determined by the annual meeting. The fee may be set to €0 (zero) for the founding period.

§ 5 — Board (Hallitus)

Board Composition

The association's affairs are managed by a board consisting of a chairperson and 2–6 other members elected at the annual meeting.

The board's term is one calendar year. The board selects from among its members a vice-chairperson, secretary, and treasurer (roles may be combined).

§ 6–8: Meetings, Fiscal Year & Signing Rights

§ 6 — Annual Meeting (Vuosikokous)

Annual General Meeting

The association's annual meeting is held each year by the end of March. At the annual meeting:

  • The board's annual report and financial statements are presented
  • Financial statements are adopted and the board is discharged
  • The membership fee for the next year is decided
  • The chairperson and board members are elected
  • An auditor (or operations inspector) is elected
§ 7 — Fiscal Year (Tilikausi)

Calendar Year

The association's fiscal year is the calendar year (1 January – 31 December).

§ 8 — Signing Rights (Nimenkirjoitusoikeus)

Representation

The association's name is signed by the chairperson alone, or by two board members together.

§ 9: Dissolution & Assets

§ 9 — Dissolution (Purkaminen)

Asset Distribution

If the association is dissolved, its remaining assets shall be used for a purpose that aligns with the association's objectives, as decided by the final meeting. Assets shall be directed to a registered non-profit organization operating in the international justice field.

Tax-exempt requirement: This clause satisfies Vero's requirement that assets of a yleishyödyllinen yhteisö (public-benefit entity) cannot be distributed to members upon dissolution.

1. Entity Types — Stichting vs Vereniging

The Netherlands offers two main non-profit legal forms. For this mission, a Stichting (Foundation) is strongly recommended.

CriteriaStichting (Foundation)Vereniging (Association)
MembersNo members — board-onlyMembership-based, democratic
EstablishmentNotarial deed requiredNotarial deed (for full legal capacity)
Min. directors1 (recommended 3+)2 members minimum
Min. capitalNone requiredNone required
Registration cost€500–€1,500 (notary fees)Similar notary costs
GovernanceBoard-driven, streamlinedMember general meeting has ultimate authority
Best forNGOs, charities, legal researchSports clubs, community groups
ANBI eligibilityYes — standard pathYes, but less common
Recommendation: A Stichting is the best fit — centralized board governance, no membership overhead, and the standard Dutch legal form for international human rights NGOs.

2. Legal Framework

  • Governing law: Book 2 of the Dutch Civil Code (Burgerlijk Wetboek)
  • Registration authority: Chamber of Commerce (KVK) — kvk.nl
  • Tax body: Belastingdienst (Dutch Tax Administration)
  • Key distinction: The Hague is the International City of Peace and Justice, home to ICC, ICJ, Permanent Court of Arbitration, Eurojust, and hundreds of international justice NGOs

3. Formation Requirements

3.1 Directors

  • Minimum 1 director (recommended: 3+ for ANBI and credibility)
  • Directors can be of any nationality — no residency requirement
  • No membership structure — board has full authority

3.2 Notarial Deed of Incorporation

A Dutch civil-law notary (notaris) must draft and execute the deed, including:

  • Articles of Association (statuten) — must be in Dutch
  • Purpose clause
  • Board composition and powers
  • Dissolution clause (assets must transfer to another ANBI)

3.3 Required Documents

Passport/EU IDFor all directors and UBOs

3.4 KVK Registration

  • Notary typically handles this
  • Processing: 2–3 working days
  • Foundation receives KVK number and is automatically notified to the Belastingdienst
  • VAT number issued within ~2 weeks if needed

4. ANBI Status (Tax Exemption)

ANBI = Algemeen Nut Beogende Instelling (Public Benefit Organisation). This is the Dutch equivalent of tax-exempt non-profit status.

Requirements

  1. 90%+ activities must serve the public interest
  2. No profit motive — surplus must be used for mission
  3. Board independence — no single person can control assets
  4. Directors' pay — must not be excessive
  5. Financial transparency — annual report, policy plan, clear accounting
  6. Anti-hoarding — must use assets for public benefit
  7. Dissolution clause — remaining assets must go to another ANBI
  8. Publication requirement — statutes, policy plan, annual report, board members on website

Benefits

BenefitDetail
Gift/inheritance tax exemptFoundation pays no tax on donations
Donor deductibilityDonors can deduct gifts from income/corporate tax
Volunteer deductionsVolunteer work can qualify as in-kind donation
CredibilityANBI status is a recognized quality mark internationally
Note: Human rights legal research clearly qualifies for ANBI status. Application is straightforward through the Belastingdienst ANBI team.

5. Funding Opportunities

5.1 Dutch Ministry of Foreign Affairs — Human Rights Fund

  • Dedicated fund for human rights protection and promotion globally
  • Distributed via embassies, competitive grant calls, and international contributions
  • Priority areas: human rights defenders, freedom of expression, equal rights
  • Applications in English
Warning: The Dutch government announced significant cuts to civil society funding starting 2026 with a "Netherlands-first" approach. Factor this into long-term planning.

5.2 EU Funding

European Commission programs for justice, rule of law, and human rights.

5.3 Private & International Foundations

  • Open Society Foundations, Oak Foundation, Sigrid Rausing Trust
  • Nationale Postcode Loterij — major human rights donor
  • Mama Cash, Hivos, and other Dutch-based human rights funders

7. The Hague — Strategic Proximity to International Justice

This is the Netherlands' most powerful advantage for this mission.

What Proximity Enables

  • Direct engagement with ICC Office of the Prosecutor — NGOs can submit evidence
  • Access to international law experts — largest concentration globally
  • Networking with The Hague Justice Portal and academic institutions
  • Easy attendance at ICC hearings, Assembly of States Parties meetings
  • Media visibility — The Hague is the default city for international justice coverage

12. Recommended Next Steps

  1. Select a Dutch notary experienced with international NGOs
  2. Draft articles of association — purpose focused on legal research and accountability
  3. Secure a Dutch registered address — virtual office or partner organization
  4. Apply for ANBI status immediately after KVK registration
  5. Open bank account — prepare all KYC/AML documentation in advance
  6. Connect with The Hague justice community — Coalition for the ICC, T.M.C. Asser Institute
  7. Apply for Dutch MFA Human Rights Fund grants (while still available)

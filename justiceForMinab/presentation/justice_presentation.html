Legal Framework

Three Pathways to Accountability

Our focused strategy targets three mutually-reinforcing legal approaches — each amplifying the others.

🟢

Public Pressure Engine

PFP-coordinated global campaigns create the political will that makes legal action possible. Without public pressure, no prosecutor will act.

🔵

ICC via Article 12(3)

Iran files an ad hoc declaration accepting ICC jurisdiction. Precedent: Palestine (2014), Ukraine. Can lead to individual arrest warrants.

🟠

Universal Jurisdiction

Criminal complaint filed in Germany under Völkerstrafgesetzbuch. No territorial connection needed. ECCHR has done this for Syria.

Each approach feeds the others: pressure (1) creates will for ICC (2) and provides evidence for Germany (3).

06 / 12

Approach 1

ICC via Iran's Article 12(3) Declaration

How It Works

  1. Iran files Article 12(3) declaration → accepts ICC jurisdiction
  2. ICC Prosecutor opens preliminary examination
  3. Pre-Trial Chamber authorizes full investigation
  4. Investigation → evidence → arrest warrants

Precedent: Palestine (2014) and Ukraine have successfully used this mechanism.

Key Charges

  • Rome Statute Article 8
    War crimes — intentional attacks on civilian objects
  • Command Responsibility
    Commanders liable if they knew or should have known
  • Precautionary Principle
    Outdated intelligence violates duty to verify targets
07 / 12

Approach 2

Universal Jurisdiction — Germany

Germany's Völkerstrafgesetzbuch allows prosecution of international crimes with zero territorial connection.

Why Germany?

  • ✓  Successfully prosecuted Syrian officials for war crimes
  • ✓  ECCHR (Berlin) has filed similar cases before
  • ✓  Doesn't require Iran's cooperation
  • ✓  Pinochet precedent: immunity ceases after leaving office

Filing Timeline

  • Month 1–2
    Build evidence package, engage ECCHR
  • Month 2–3
    Submit criminal complaint to Generalbundesanwalt
  • Month 3–12+
    Support prosecutor with evidence, witnesses

# Establishing a Non-Profit NGO in the Netherlands

> **Purpose:** Legal research and documentation for the Justice for Minab Children campaign, building a case for a future international court of justice proceeding.

---

## 1. Entity Types — Stichting vs Vereniging

The Netherlands offers two main non-profit legal forms. For this mission, a **Stichting (Foundation)** is strongly recommended.

| Criteria | Stichting (Foundation) | Vereniging (Association) |
|---|---|---|
| **Members** | No members — board-only | Membership-based, democratic |
| **Establishment** | Notarial deed required | Notarial deed (for full legal capacity) |
| **Min. directors** | 1 (recommended 3+) | 2 members minimum |
| **Min. capital** | None required | None required |
| **Registration cost** | €500–€1,500 (notary fees) + KVK fee | Similar notary costs |
| **Governance** | Board-driven, streamlined | Member general meeting has ultimate authority |
| **Best for** | NGOs, charities, legal research | Sports clubs, community groups |
| **ANBI eligibility** | Yes — standard path | Yes, but less common |

> [!TIP]
> **Recommendation:** A **Stichting** is the best fit — it gives centralized board governance, no membership overhead, and is the standard Dutch legal form for international human rights NGOs. It maps directly to how organizations like Amnesty, HRW, and ECCHR operate.

## 2. Legal Framework

- **Governing law:** Book 2 of the Dutch Civil Code (Burgerlijk Wetboek)
- **Registration authority:** Chamber of Commerce (KVK) — [kvk.nl](https://kvk.nl)
- **Tax body:** Belastingdienst (Dutch Tax Administration)
- **Key distinction:** The Hague is the **International City of Peace and Justice**, home to ICC, ICJ, Permanent Court of Arbitration, Eurojust, and hundreds of international justice NGOs

---

## 3. Formation Requirements

### 3.1 Directors
- **Minimum 1 director** (recommended: 3+ for ANBI status and governance credibility)
- Directors **can be of any nationality** — no residency requirement
- No membership structure — board has full authority

### 3.2 Notarial Deed of Incorporation
A Dutch civil-law notary (**notaris**) must draft and execute the deed. This includes:
- **Articles of Association** (statuten) — must be in Dutch
- Purpose clause
- Board composition and powers
- Dissolution clause (assets must transfer to another ANBI or similar public-benefit entity)

### 3.3 Required Documents

| Document | Details |
|---|---|
| **Passport/EU ID** | For all directors and UBOs |
| **Proof of address** | Utility bill or bank statement for each director |
| **Deed of Incorporation** | Prepared by Dutch notary |
| **Dutch office address** | Physical or virtual registered address in NL |
| **UBO registration** | Mandatory in KVK register |

### 3.4 KVK Registration
- Notary typically handles this
- Processing: **2–3 working days**
- Foundation receives **KVK number** and is automatically notified to the Belastingdienst
- VAT number issued within ~2 weeks if needed

## 4. ANBI Status (Tax Exemption)

**ANBI** = Algemeen Nut Beogende Instelling (Public Benefit Organisation)

This is the Dutch equivalent of tax-exempt non-profit status. **Highly recommended** for the NGO.

### Requirements
1. **90%+ activities** must serve the public interest
2. **No profit motive** — surplus must be used for mission
3. **Board independence** — no single person can control assets or hold majority vote
4. **Directors' pay** — must not be excessive (transparent remuneration policy)
5. **Financial transparency** — annual report, policy plan, clear accounting
6. **Anti-hoarding** — must use assets for public benefit, not accumulate unnecessarily
7. **Dissolution clause** — remaining assets must go to another ANBI
8. **Publication requirement** — must publish on website: statutes, policy plan, annual report, board members, financial statements

### Benefits
| Benefit | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Gift/inheritance tax exempt** | Foundation pays no tax on donations |
| **Donor deductibility** | Donors can deduct gifts from income/corporate tax |
| **Volunteer deductions** | Volunteer work can qualify as in-kind donation |
| **Credibility** | ANBI status is a recognized quality mark internationally |

> [!NOTE]
> Human rights legal research clearly qualifies for ANBI status given its public benefit nature. Application is straightforward through the Belastingdienst ANBI team.

## 5. Funding Opportunities

### 5.1 Dutch Ministry of Foreign Affairs — Human Rights Fund
- Dedicated fund for human rights protection and promotion globally
- Distributed via embassies, competitive grant calls, and international organization contributions
- Priority areas: human rights defenders, freedom of expression, equal rights
- Applications in English
- **Example:** "Safety for Voices" (2022–2027), "Focus on Freedom" (2026–2030)

> [!WARNING]
> The Dutch government announced **significant cuts to civil society funding starting 2026** with a "Netherlands-first" approach. This may reduce available grants for international human rights work. Factor this into long-term planning.

### 5.2 EU Funding
- European Commission programs for justice, rule of law, and human rights
- Netherlands-based NGOs have strong access to EU funding channels

### 5.3 Private & International Foundations
- Open Society Foundations, Oak Foundation, Sigrid Rausing Trust
- Dutch lottery funds (Nationale Postcode Loterij — major human rights donor)
- Mama Cash, Hivos, and other Dutch-based human rights funders

## 6. Banking & Operations

| Item | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Banks** | ABN AMRO, ING, Rabobank — all serve stichtingen |
| **Documents** | KVK extract, Articles of Association, director IDs, business plan |
| **Foreign directors** | Allowed — enhanced KYC/CDD due diligence applies |
| **Processing** | 1–4 weeks (potentially 3–6 weeks for foreign-owned) |
| **Dutch address** | Required for bank account opening |
| **Language** | Articles must be in Dutch; operational docs can be English |

> [!IMPORTANT]
> Dutch banks apply **strict AML scrutiny** for stichtingen with foreign directors. Prepare a comprehensive business plan, source-of-funds documentation, and evidence of Dutch "economic substance" (local activities, address, clients).

## 7. The Hague — Strategic Proximity to International Justice

This is the Netherlands' **most powerful advantage** for this mission.

| Institution | Location | Relevance |
|---|---|---|
| **ICC** (International Criminal Court) | The Hague | Direct jurisdiction over war crimes, crimes against humanity |
| **ICJ** (International Court of Justice) | Peace Palace, The Hague | State-to-state disputes, advisory opinions |
| **Permanent Court of Arbitration** | Peace Palace | Alternative dispute resolution |
| **Eurojust** | The Hague | EU judicial cooperation for serious crime |
| **Coalition for the ICC** | The Hague | 2,500+ NGO network supporting ICC |
| **Hundreds of justice NGOs** | The Hague | HRW, ICJ, FIDH all have Hague offices |
| **T.M.C. Asser Institute** | The Hague | International law research and education |

### What Proximity Enables
- **Direct engagement with ICC Office of the Prosecutor** — NGOs can submit evidence and communications
- **Access to international law experts** — largest concentration globally
- **Networking** with The Hague Justice Portal and academic institutions
- **Easy attendance** at ICC hearings, Assembly of States Parties meetings
- **Media visibility** — The Hague is the default city for international justice media coverage

## 10. Potential Challenges

| Challenge | Mitigation |
|---|---|
| **Notary required** | €500–€1,500 upfront cost; cannot self-register |
| **Articles must be in Dutch** | Notary handles translation; operational docs in English |
| **Banking delays** | Enhanced KYC for foreign directors; start process immediately |
| **2026 funding cuts** | Diversify to EU, private foundations, and crowdfunding |
| **No democratic structure** | Stichting has no members — less community engagement by design |
| **AML scrutiny** | Prepare thorough source-of-funds documentation |

## 12. Recommended Next Steps (Netherlands Track)

1. **Select a Dutch notary** — preferably one experienced with international NGOs/stichtingen
2. **Draft articles of association** — purpose statement focused on legal research, evidence documentation, and accountability for the Minab school attack
3. **Secure a Dutch registered address** — virtual office or partner organization
4. **Apply for ANBI status** immediately after KVK registration
5. **Open bank account** — prepare all KYC/AML documentation in advance
6. **Connect with The Hague justice community** — Coalition for the ICC, T.M.C. Asser Institute
7. **Apply for Dutch MFA Human Rights Fund** grants (while still available)

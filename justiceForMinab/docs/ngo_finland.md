# Establishing a Non-Profit NGO in Finland

> **Purpose:** Legal research and documentation for the Justice for Minab Children campaign, building a case for a future international court of justice proceeding.

---

## 1. Entity Types — Association vs Foundation

Finland offers two main non-profit legal forms. For this mission, a **Registered Association (yhdistys / ry)** is strongly recommended over a Foundation.

| Criteria | Association (Yhdistys / ry) | Foundation (Säätiö) |
|---|---|---|
| **Minimum capital** | None | €50,000 (recommended €1M+) |
| **Registration fee** | €55 online / €110 paper | €920–€1,680 |
| **Processing time** | ~2 working days (online) | Longer, more complex review |
| **Members** | Yes — open membership | No members |
| **Governance** | Democratic, member-led | Board-only, rigid purpose |
| **Flexibility** | High — purpose can evolve | Low — purpose is fixed |
| **Best for** | Advocacy, research, campaigns | Grant-making, endowments |

> [!TIP]
> **Recommendation:** A **Registered Association (ry)** is the right choice — low cost, fast setup, democratic governance, and full flexibility for legal research, advocacy, and public engagement.

## 2. Legal Framework

- **Governing law:** Associations Act (Yhdistyslaki 503/1989, as amended)
- **Registration authority:** Finnish Patent and Registration Office (PRH) — [prh.fi](https://prh.fi)
- **Constitutional protection:** Freedom of association is guaranteed under Section 13(2) of the Finnish Constitution and reinforced by ICCPR and ECHR (both ratified by Finland)
- **Landscape:** Finland has 123,000+ registered associations with 15M+ total memberships in a country of 5.5M people — one of the highest civic participation rates globally

## 3. Formation Requirements

### 3.1 Founders
- **Minimum 3 founders** (natural persons aged 15+ or legal entities)
- Foreigners **can** be founders and board members
- **Chairperson must reside in Finland** (or obtain PRH exemption)

### 3.2 Founding Meeting
The founders convene and:
1. Adopt the association's **rules (säännöt)**
2. Elect the **board of directors**
3. Sign the **founding charter (perustamiskirja)**
4. Approve the registration application

### 3.3 Rules (Säännöt) — Required Content
Must be in **Finnish or Swedish** (bilingual version acceptable for international members). Must include:

| Item | Details |
|---|---|
| **Name** | Must be unique. Suffix "ry" added upon registration |
| **Domicile** | A municipality in Finland |
| **Purpose** | Non-profit, lawful, specific — e.g., *"Legal research, documentation, and international advocacy for accountability in the Minab school attack"* |
| **Membership** | Conditions for joining and fees (if any) |
| **Board** | Minimum: chairperson (18+) + 2 members (15+) |
| **Accounting period** | Fiscal year definition |
| **Meeting procedures** | How to convene, notice period |
| **Dissolution clause** | Assets must go to a similar non-profit purpose |

### 3.4 Registration with PRH

| Step | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Method** | Online (recommended) or paper |
| **Cost** | **€55** online / €110 paper |
| **Processing** | ~2 working days (online) |
| **ID requirement** | Signer needs Finnish personal ID + bank/mobile ID for online filing |
| **Documents** | Rules, founding charter, board details, minutes |

> [!IMPORTANT]
> Online filing requires **Finnish personal identity codes** and Finnish e-ID (bank credentials or mobile certificate). If all founders are foreign, **paper filing** (€110) or a Finnish resident co-founder is needed.

## 4. Tax Status — Non-Profit Exemption

To qualify as *yleishyödyllinen yhteisö* (public-benefit entity) under Finnish tax law:

1. **Operates exclusively for the public good** (material, intellectual, moral, or societal)
2. **Activities are not limited to a closed group** — must benefit the broader public
3. **No financial benefit to participants** — no dividends, profit-sharing, or excessive salaries

### Tax-Exempt Income (if qualified)
- Membership fees
- Donations and grants
- Dividends and interest income
- Subsidies for non-profit activities
- Most capital gains

### Taxable Income
- Business activity income → **20% corporate tax**
- Certain real estate income

> [!NOTE]
> An NGO focused on legal research and human rights documentation clearly fits the public-benefit criteria. The Finnish Tax Administration (Vero) assesses this on a case-by-case basis based on both rules and actual operations.

## 5. Funding Opportunities

### 5.1 Ministry for Foreign Affairs (MFA)
- **Grants for human rights NGOs** — both Finnish and international
- Thematic priorities: human rights, gender equality, democracy, accountability
- Example: In 2024, €10M awarded to 9 INGOs for human rights work (2025–2026)
- Also provides **multi-annual funding** for CSOs in development cooperation
- Website: [um.fi](https://um.fi)

### 5.2 STEA (Funding Centre for Social Welfare & Health)
- Primarily domestic social welfare — less directly relevant to this mission
- Still possible if framed around community support for affected diaspora families

### 5.3 EU Funding
- European Commission grants for human rights, justice, and rule of law programs
- Horizon Europe for research-oriented projects

### 5.4 Private & Foundation Grants
- KIOS Foundation (Finnish NGO Foundation for Human Rights) — funds human rights projects
- Open Society Foundations, Sigrid Jusélius Foundation, etc.

| Item | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Bank account** | Required for operations; Finnish banks (OP, Nordea, S-Pankki) |
| **Documents needed** | Association registration extract, board minutes, ID of signatories |
| **Language** | Documents accepted in Finnish, Swedish, or English |

## 8. Universal Jurisdiction in Finland

Finland's legal system **supports universal jurisdiction** for international crimes:

- National courts can investigate and prosecute **genocide, crimes against humanity, war crimes, and torture** committed abroad
- Finnish Criminal Code Chapter 1 provides for extraterritorial jurisdiction
- Finland actively supports the ICC and advocates for universal acknowledgment of ICJ jurisdiction
- Open Society Justice Initiative and TRIAL International have published detailed analyses of Finland's universal jurisdiction framework

> [!TIP]
> This is strategically valuable: a Finnish-based NGO can not only prepare documentation but potentially support **Finnish prosecutors** in exercising universal jurisdiction over the Minab school attack.

## 10. Potential Challenges

| Challenge | Mitigation |
|---|---|
| **Chairperson must reside in Finland** | Appoint a Finnish-resident co-founder or apply for PRH exemption |
| **Rules must be in Finnish/Swedish** | Use legal translation service; operational docs can be in English |
| **Online filing needs Finnish e-ID** | Use paper filing (€110) or a Finnish-resident signer |
| **Banking delays for foreign boards** | Start process early; prepare all KYC documents in advance |
| **Limited proximity to ICC/ICJ** | Complement with Netherlands presence (see Netherlands comparison) |

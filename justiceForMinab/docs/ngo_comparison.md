## Strategic Analysis

### When to Choose 🇫🇮 Finland
- You have a **Finnish-resident co-founder** available
- You need to be **operational within days** at minimal cost
- The NGO's primary work is **research and documentation** (not direct court engagement)
- You want **stable government funding** through Finnish MFA grants
- You value the **highest rule-of-law credibility** for published reports
- Finland is a **stepping stone** — you can expand to NL later

### When to Choose 🇳🇱 Netherlands
- The core mission is **building a case for ICC/ICJ proceedings**
- You need **direct access** to ICC prosecutors, international law firms, and justice organizations
- You want **maximum international visibility** — The Hague is synonymous with justice
- **Strong ANBI tax status** with clear donor deduction framework is important
- No founding member resides in Finland (Netherlands has **no residency requirement**)
- You already have legal contacts in the Netherlands

---

## Recommendation

> [!IMPORTANT]
> **Primary recommendation: 🇳🇱 Netherlands (Stichting)**
> 
> Given that the NGO's explicit purpose is to build legal documentation for a **future court of justice proceeding**, the Netherlands is the stronger choice. The Hague's unique position as the world capital of international justice provides:
> - Direct ICC/ICJ access for evidence submission
> - The densest network of international law experts globally
> - Maximum credibility when presenting to courts and legal organizations
> - No residency requirement for board members
> 
> The slightly higher setup cost (€500–€1,500 vs €55) is negligible compared to the strategic advantages.

> [!TIP]
> **Consider a dual-entity strategy:**
> - **Primary entity:** Stichting in the Netherlands (legal engagement, court filings, evidence submission)
> - **Secondary entity:** Registered Association in Finland (research, documentation, public campaigns, MFA-funded work)
> 
> This gives you the best of both worlds: The Hague's justice ecosystem for legal proceedings + Finland's superior rule-of-law credibility and stable government funding for research output.

## Quick Reference: Setup Checklist

### Netherlands Stichting (if chosen)
1. ☐ Select Dutch notary experienced with international NGOs
2. ☐ Draft articles of association (purpose + dissolution clause)
3. ☐ Secure Dutch registered address (virtual office OK)
4. ☐ Execute notarial deed → KVK registration (~2–3 days)
5. ☐ Apply for ANBI status at Belastingdienst
6. ☐ Open bank account (ABN AMRO/ING/Rabobank)
7. ☐ Connect with Coalition for the ICC + Hague justice network

### Finland Association (if chosen or as secondary)
1. ☐ Identify Finnish-resident chairperson
2. ☐ Draft rules (säännöt) in Finnish
3. ☐ Convene founding meeting (3+ founders)
4. ☐ File with PRH (€55 online)
5. ☐ Open bank account
6. ☐ Apply for MFA human rights grants

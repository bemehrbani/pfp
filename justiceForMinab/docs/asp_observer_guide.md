## What is the ASP?

The Assembly of States Parties (ASP) is the management oversight and legislative body of the International Criminal Court. It meets annually (typically December) and is the primary venue where NGOs can:

- Make statements during the General Debate
- Host side events
- Network with state delegations
- Advocate for ICC investigations and cases

## How to Get NGO Observer Accreditation

### Prerequisites
1. ✅ Legally registered organization (KVK registration is sufficient)
2. ✅ Demonstrated work in international criminal justice / human rights
3. ✅ Non-profit status documentation
4. ✅ Organization website with mission, activities, and contact info

### Application Process
1. **Contact the Coalition for the ICC (CICC)** — they coordinate civil society participation
   - Website: coalitionfortheicc.org
   - They maintain a database of accredited NGOs
   - First-time organizations typically go through CICC

2. **Submit to the ASP Secretariat:**
   - Letter of application signed by the head of the organization
   - Description of organization's identity, aims, objectives
   - Proof of legal status (KVK excerpt)
   - Summary of activities relevant to ICC/international justice
   - List of representatives who will attend

3. **Deadline:** Typically 2–3 months before the session. For ASP 25 (Dec 2026), submit by **September 2026**.

### What Accredited NGOs Can Do
- Attend plenary sessions as observers
- Make oral/written statements during General Debate
- Organize side events (requires separate application)
- Access to official documents
- Network in the margins with delegations

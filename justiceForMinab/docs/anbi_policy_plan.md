# ANBI Policy Plan (Beleidsplan) — Research & Template

> **Purpose:** Template and checklist for the required ANBI policy plan  
> **Last updated:** March 15, 2026  
> **Source:** Belastingdienst (Dutch Tax Authority) requirements

---

## What is a Beleidsplan?

Every Dutch ANBI (Algemeen Nut Beogende Instelling) must maintain a current policy plan. The Belastingdienst uses it to verify that the organization genuinely serves the general interest and manages its assets responsibly.

**Key rule (2026):** The new Wet Transparantie Maatschappelijke Organisaties (Wtmo), effective **January 1, 2026**, requires stichtingen to submit financial data to the KVK. This means stricter oversight of reserves and spending.

## Required Elements

### 1. Objectives & Activities (Doelen & Werkzaamheden)
*What the foundation aims to achieve and how:*

- Legal research and documentation of human rights violations
- Evidence-gathering for international judicial proceedings
- Public advocacy campaigns (social media, press, political lobbying)
- Partnerships with ICC, ICJ-adjacent organizations
- Victim support coordination
- Publications and educational materials

### 2. Income Generation (Inkomstenverwerving)
*How the foundation will acquire its funding:*

- Individual donations (online, campaigns)
- Grant applications (EU, UN, Dutch gov't via MFA)
- Institutional partnerships
- Crowdfunding campaigns
- Pro-bono legal services (received, not charged)

### 3. Asset Management (Vermogensbeheer)
*How collected funds are managed:*

- Operating account at a Dutch bank
- Board-approved annual budget
- Dual-signature requirement for expenditures over €X
- Annual financial report published on website
- No investments in securities (initial phase)

### 4. Expenditure Plan (Bestedingsbeleid)
*How the funds will be spent:*

| Category | Estimated % | Examples |
|----------|-------------|---------|
| Legal research & documentation | 40% | Case files, expert consultations, translations |
| Advocacy & campaigns | 25% | Media, web, social media, events |
| Operations | 20% | Address, bank, insurance, admin |
| Partnerships & travel | 10% | Conferences, ICC/ASP engagement |
| Reserve (continuity) | 5% | Emergency buffer |

### 5. Anti-Hoarding Rule (Anti-oppoteis) — NEW 2025/2026
*Critical: The foundation cannot retain more assets than reasonably necessary.*

- **Maximum continuity reserve:** 1.5x annual operating costs
- **All reserves must be justified** in writing in the policy plan
- **Earmarked funds** (doelvermogen) for specific projects are allowed but must have clear timelines
- Belastingdienst will scrutinize foundations holding excessive cash

### 6. Timeline for Activities
*Required since 2025 ANBI policy update:*

| Quarter | Planned Activities |
|---------|-------------------|
| Q2 2026 | Stichting formation, board confirmation, KVK registration |
| Q3 2026 | ANBI application, bank account, website ANBI page |
| Q4 2026 | First case documentation work, ASP 25 observer application |
| Q1 2027 | First publication, grant applications |

## What Must Be Published on the Website

The ANBI requires the following information to be publicly available on the foundation's website:

1. ✅ Name of the foundation
2. ✅ RSIN / fiscal number (assigned after KVK registration)
3. ✅ Contact details
4. ✅ Purpose and mission (from the statuten)
5. ✅ Policy plan summary
6. ✅ Board composition and their functions
7. ✅ Remuneration policy (bestuurders ontvangen geen beloning)
8. ✅ Annual financial report (jaarrekening)
9. ✅ Annual activity report (jaarverslag)

> **Note:** Board members of an ANBI cannot receive compensation beyond reasonable expense reimbursement.

## Template Outline for Submission

```
BELEIDSPLAN [Foundation Name]
Period: 2026–2029

1. INLEIDING (Introduction)
   - Foundation background and context
   - Justice for Minab Children campaign

2. DOELSTELLING (Mission & Purpose)
   - As per Article 2 of the Statuten
   
3. WERKZAAMHEDEN (Activities)
   - Research and documentation
   - Advocacy and campaigns  
   - Partnerships and coalition building
   - Victim support

4. ORGANISATIESTRUCTUUR (Organization)
   - Board composition and roles
   - No remuneration for board members
   - Volunteer structure

5. WERVINGSBELEID (Fundraising)
   - Donation sources
   - Grant applications
   - Cost of fundraising vs. proceeds

6. FINANCIEEL BEHEER (Financial Management)
   - Budget overview
   - Reserve policy (max 1.5x annual costs)
   - Expenditure categories

7. BESTEDINGSBELEID (Spending Policy)
   - How resources are allocated
   - Decision-making process

8. BIJLAGEN (Appendices)
   - Board member details
   - Budget spreadsheet
   - Founding deed reference
```
